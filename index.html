Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao, widely known as NTR or Sr. NTR, is a legendary figure in Indian cinema and politics, particularly famous in the Telugu-speaking states of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. Born on May 28, 1923, he became one of the most beloved actors in Telugu cinema and later transformed into a highly influential political leader.

Early Life and Entry into Cinema

NTR was born in Nimmakuru, a small village in the Krishna district of Andhra Pradesh. He graduated with a diploma in arts before entering the film industry in 1949 with the movie Mana Desam. Over his 33-year film career, NTR acted in over 300 movies, taking on iconic roles in mythology-based films and earning admiration for portraying Hindu deities like Lord Krishna, Rama, and Vishnu. His strong screen presence and memorable voice helped him connect deeply with audiences.

Acting Career and Iconic Roles

NTR is known for his versatility, having played a wide range of roles, including mythological, historical, and social characters. His portrayals in films like Maya Bazaar (as Krishna), Daana Veera Soora Karna (as Karna), Lava Kusa, Bobbili Puli, and Pathala Bhairavi set benchmarks in Indian cinema. His charismatic portrayal of deities helped him gain a devoted fan following, and he became seen as a larger-than-life figure.

Political Career and Legacy

In 1982, NTR founded the Telugu Desam Party (TDP) with a mission to uphold Telugu pride and identity. He rapidly gained popularity, campaigning on the idea of "self-respect for Telugu people" and challenging the established political norms. His political style was unique; he traveled in a Chaitanya Ratham, a van he used for extensive tours to connect with the public.Within nine months of forming the TDP, he led his party to a historic victory in the 1983 Andhra Pradesh state elections, becoming the Chief Minister. He introduced several welfare programs aimed at benefiting the poor, such as Subsidized Rice Scheme and Women's Empowerment Programs, which made a lasting impact on the region.NTR’s political career had its share of turbulence, including a brief removal from office and a dramatic comeback. However, his influence on regional politics remained monumental. He passed away on January 18, 1996, but his legacy endures both in cinema and in the Telugu states' political landscape.

Legacy and Influence

NTR is remembered as a symbol of Telugu pride, and his contributions to cinema and politics have made him a revered figure. His grandson, Jr. NTR, and son, Nandamuri Balakrishna, have followed in his footsteps in the film industry, continuing his cinematic legacy. NTR’s life and legacy were also depicted in a biographical film released in two parts, NTR: Kathanayakudu and NTR: Mahanayakudu, showcasing his journey from cinema to politics.
